CLASS 5 LICENCE

What Is a Class 5 License?

A Class 5 license is the most common type of driver's license in Manitoba. It allows you to drive:

  • Passenger vehicles (cars, SUVs, minivans)
  • Light trucks and utility vehicles
  • Small delivery vehicles (non-commercial)
GDL PROGRAM

Manitoba's Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL) System

We support you through all three stages of Manitoba's Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL) program:

  • Learner Stage (L): Written test preparation, traffic rules, and basic vehicle handling.
  • Intermediate Stage (I): In-depth training, road safety, hazard perception, and city driving.
  • Full License (F): Advanced driving techniques, highway training, winter driving, and more.

Career Opportunities with a Class 5 License

A Class 5 license in Winnipeg opens the door to:

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I apply for a Class 5 license in Manitoba?

You start by passing a written knowledge test and vision test at an MPI location. From there, you progress through the GDL stages with supervised driving and a road test.

How long does it take to get a Full Class 5 license?

It generally takes 15 months from Learner (L) to Intermediate (I), and another 15 months to earn a Full Class 5 license, depending on your age and driving record.

Can I take the Class 5 test in winter?

Yes. We offer winter driving lessons to prepare you for icy roads, low visibility, and defensive winter driving techniques.

Do you offer lessons in other languages?

Yes. We offer driving lessons in English, Punjabi, Hindi, and other languages upon request.
